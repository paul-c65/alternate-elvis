RCA 47  7740                        Black Label – Dog On Top

 

Side A.  Stuck On You

Side B.  Fame And Fortune

 

"Stuck On You" was number 1 on the Hot 100 chart for four weeks, it was on the chart for fourteen weeks, it numbered 6 on the R&B chart, it remained on this chart for nine weeks. "Fame And Fortune" numbered 17 on the Hot 100 chart, and remained on the chart for ten weeks. With advance orders of 1,275,077 copies Elvis’ first post-army single set a new record, so “Stuck On You” was Elvis’ 25th million seller, before it had even been recorded. Since RCA wanted the single out fast, a sleeve without song titles was prepared. A die-cut hole showed the label with the titles. This was the first Elvis single to be released in both mono and stereo This 45 was released in the UK in March 1956 on His Master's Voice Label, catalogue No.7m 385, it had gold lettering on a purple label, and was later done in silver lettering and reached the number 2 slot.

 

Matrix numbers: L2WW-0083-13S A4 / L2WW-0084-13S A2 A

This was also released in stereo Stuck On You / Fame and Fortune in 1960.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Stuck On You / Fame and Fortune on the Gold Standard Series label.

 

Recording Dates

Stuck On You - Recorded March 21st 1960, RCA Studio’s Nashville, Tennessee

Fame And Fortune - Recorded March 21st 1960, RCA Studio’s Nashville, Tennessee
