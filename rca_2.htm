RCA 47  7000                        Black Label – Dog On Top

 Side A.  Teddy Bear

Side B.  Loving You

 On June 19th 1957 the Hot 100 chart had Teddy Bear in the number 1 position, it remained number 1 for seven weeks and stayed on the chart for twenty five weeks, the Country chart had this track at the number 1 slot on June 22nd, and remained on the chart for sixteen weeks. The Rhythm & Blues chart also had the song at number 1 on June 29th, it stayed on this chart for twelve weeks. Loving You was on Billboard's Hot 100 chart on June 19th at the number 28 position and stayed on the chart for twenty two weeks. On June 29th the Rhythm & Blues chart had this song at number 1, where it remained on the chart for twelve weeks.


This was released in the UK by RCA, catalogue No RCA 1013, with a black label with a triangular centre, this 45 reached number 3 in the UK charts in July 1957.

Matrix numbers: H2WW-2193-2S D2 / H2WW-0418-1S B4

Released in 1957 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as (Let Me Be Your) Teddy Bear / Loving You.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as (Let Me Be Your) Teddy Bear / Loving You on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

Teddy Bear - Recorded 18th January 1957, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

Loving You - Recorded 24th February 1957, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

 

RCA 47  7035                        Black Label – Dog On Top

Side A.  Jailhouse Rock

Side B.  Treat Me Nice

Jailhouse Rock entered the Hot 100 at number 1, it stayed number 1 for six weeks and was on the chart for twenty seven weeks. On the same day the Country chart also had this song at number 1, and it remained on this chart for twenty four weeks, it also entered the Rhythm & Blues chart at number 1, also on the same day and stayed on the chart for twelve weeks. The Hot 100 chart had Treat Me Nice in the number 27 slot on October 12th, it stayed on the chart for ten weeks, on October 5th the song was number 1 on the Rhythm & Blues chart where it stayed for fifteen weeks.

RCA released this single in January 1958, catalogue No. RCA 1028, it reached number 1 on the UK Chart.

Matrix numbers: H2WW-6779-1S B4 / HW22-6778-1S A2 (machine stamped)

Released in 1957 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as Jailhouse Rock / Treat Me Nice.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Jailhouse Rock / Treat Me Nice on the Gold Standard Series label.

 Recording Dates

Jailhouse Rock - Recorded 30th April 1957, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

Treat Me Nice - Recorded 3rd May 1957, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

 

RCA 47  7150                        Black Label – Dog On Top

Side A.  Don’t

Side B.  I Beg Of You

Don't was number 1 on the Hot 100 chat when it entered on January 7th 1958, it remained on the chart for twenty weeks. On January 25th this song entered both the Country & Rhythm & Blues chart, it was number 2 on the Country chart, it stayed on this chart for eighteen weeks and number 4 on the Rhythm & Blues chart where it stayed on the chart for ten weeks. 

I Beg Of You entered the Hot 100 chart on January 7th at the number 8 slot, this also stayed on the chart for twenty weeks, the Country chart had this track at number 2 on January 25th, it remained for eighteen weeks, it was number 4 on the Rhythm & Blues chart remaining on the chart for ten weeks.

Released in the UK by RCA in February 1958 and reached the number 2 slot in the chart, catalogue No. RCA 1043, with black label and a triangular centre, and later released with round centers.

Matrix numbers: H2PW-5529-5S A7 / H2WW-0259-5S B6

Released in 1958 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as Don't / I Beg of You.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Don't / I Beg of You on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

Don't - Recorded 6th September 1957, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

I Beg Of You - Recorded 23rd February 1957, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

 

RCA 47  7240                        Black Label – Dog On Top

Side A.  Wear My Ring Around Your Neck

Side B.  Doncha’ Think It’s Time

Wear My Ring Around Your Neck entered both the Hot 100 and the Country charts for fifteen weeks, on April 7th, it was number 2 on the Hot 100 chart and number 3 on the Country chat and at the number 1 slot on the Rhythm & Blues chart where it stayed for eleven weeks. Doncha' Think It's Time made number 21 on the same day and remained on the chart for six weeks.

April 1958 was the release date for this 45 in the UK, it reached number 3 on the UK chart, it has a black label and also has a triangular centre, and later released with round centers, catalogue No. RCA 1058.

Matrix numbers: J2WW-0179-1S A1 / J2WW-0181-8S A1

Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Wear My Ring Around Your Neck / Doncha' Think It's Time on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

Wear My Ring Around Your Neck - Recorded 1st February 1958, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

Doncha' Think It's Time - Recorded 1st February 1958, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

 

RCA 47  7280                        Black Label – Dog On Top

Side A.  Hard Headed Woman

Side B.  Don’t Ask Me Why

On June 10th 1958 Hard Headed Woman entered Billboard's Hot 100 at the number 1 slot, on the same day it entered both the Country & Rhythm & Blues charts at the number 2 position, it remained for thirteen weeks on the Hot 100, sixteen on the Country and ten on the R&B charts. Don't Ask Me Why was number 28 on the Hot 100 chart on June 28th, it remained on this chart for nine weeks, it was number 2 on the R&B chart where it remained for ten weeks.
RCA released this 45 in the UK in July 1958, it would reach the number 2 slot on the chart, it has a black label and a triangular centre, and later released with round centers, catalogue No. RCA 1070.

Matrix numbers: J2PW-3603-1S A1 / J2PW-3610-4S A1

Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Hard Headed Woman / Don't Ask Me Why on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

Hard Headed Woman - Recorded 15th February 1958, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

Don't Ask Me Why - Recorded 16th February 1958, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

 

RCA 47  7410                        Black Label – Dog On Top

 

Side A.  One Night

Side B.  I Got Stung

 

One Night numbered 4 on the Hot 100 chart on November 16th 1958, it remained on the chart for seventeen weeks. On December 28th the Country chart listed the song at number 24, and remained on the chart for three weeks, this track held the number 10 slot on the Rhythm & Blues chart on November 23rd where it stayed for fifteen weeks. Billboard listed I Got Stung at number 8 on November 9th 1958, it remained on this chart for sixteen weeks.


Released January 1959 by RCA in the UK, this 45 reached number one on the UK chart, it has a black label and a triangular centre, catalogue No. RCA 1100. This was the last Elvis record to be released as a 78.

Matrix numbers: H2WW-0415-3S A1 / H2WW-3257-2S (machine stamped)

Released in 1958 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as One Night / I Got Stung.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as One Night / I Got Stung on the Gold Standard Series label.

 

Recording Dates

One Night - Recorded 23rd February 1958, Radio Recorders, Hollywood

I Got Stung - Recorded 10th June 1958, RCA Studio B, Nashville

 

RCA 47  7506                        Black Label – Dog On Top

 

Side A.  A Fool Such As I

Side B.  I Need Your Love Tonight

 

A Fool Such As I entered Billboard's Hot 100 chart on March 10th 1959 in the number 2 slot, it stayed on the chart for fifteen weeks, on April 19th this song held the number 16 position on the Rhythm & Blues chart, where it stayed for the next seven weeks. Billboard listed I Need Your Love Tonight on it's Hot 100 chart on April 5th at the number 4 slot and it remained on this chart for thirteen weeks.


This was released in the UK by RCA in April 1959,and reached number one on the UK chart, the difference between the US and UK 45's is that the UK 45 has A Fool Such As I as the A-side, this has a black label and a triangular centre, and later released with round centers, catalogue No. RCA 1113.

Matrix numbers: J2WW-3253-1S / J2WW-3256-1S (machine stamped)

Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as I Need Your Love Tonight / (Now and Then There's) A Fool Such As I on the Gold Standard Series label.

 

Recording Dates

A Fool Such As I - Recorded 10th June 1958, RCA Studio B, Nashville

I Need Your Love Tonight - Recorded 10th June 1958, RCA Studio B, Nashville

 

RCA 47  7600                        Black Label – Dog On Top

 

Side A.  A Big Hunk O’Love

Side B.  My Wish Came True

 

A Big Hunk O'Love was placed number 1 on June 23rd 1959 on the Hot 100 chart and stayed on the chart for fourteen weeks. The Hot 100 placed My Wish Came True at the number 12 on July 7th, it stayed on the chart for eleven weeks, the Rhythm & Blues chart listed this track at the number 15 slot, it stayed on the chart for three weeks.


Number 4 was the position this 45 held in July 1959 in the UK, released on the RCA label, catalogue No. RCA 1136, it has a black label and a triangular centre, and later released with round centers.

Matrix numbers: J2WW-3254-1S A6 / H2PW-5524-2S A5

Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as A Big Hunk O' Love / My Wish Came True on the Gold Standard Series label.

 

Recording Dates

A Big Hunk O'Love - Recorded 10th June 1958, RCA Studio B, Nashville

My Wish Came True - Recorded 1st February 1958, Radio Recorders
