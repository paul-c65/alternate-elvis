Sun 209                 Yellow Sun Label

The label has Elvis Presley on one line, Scotty and Bill on another.

Release number at bottom of the label

 Side A.  That's All Right (mama)

Side B.  Blue Moon Of Kentucky

 Elvis' first 45, “That’s All Right” is now considered a classic and has been acknowledged a million-seller over the years, Elvis' debut single originally sold less than 20,000 copies and  didn’t enter any national charts. On the local Memphis chart, “That’s All Right” reached the number 4 position and in September “Blue Moon Of Kentucky” hit number 1.

 

 

Matrix numbers: U-128-45-72/U-129-45-72

Variations: There are at least four variations of the Blue Moon of Kentucky side of the label. From left to right, the more notable variations. Wabash Peer far left, 209 on bottom. The second is Peer BMI with upside 209 on left side. The third copy is Peer BMI with 209 on left side

Variations: There are at least four variations of the That's All Right side of the label. From left to right, the more notable variations. Wabash BMI far left, 209 on bottom. The second is Wabash Music Co. BMI on far left, 209 on bottom. The third copy is Wabash, BMI, 209 on far left side. The forth copy is Wabash Music, BMI, and 209 on far left side

Released in 1954 by Sun Records on 78 RPM as That's All Right / Blue Moon of Kentucky.
Released in 1955 by RCA Records on 45 RPM as That's All Right / Blue Moon of Kentucky.
Released in 1955 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as That's All Right / Blue Moon of Kentucky.
Released in 1959 by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Blue Moon of Kentucky / That's All Right on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

That's All Right (mama) - Recorded July 5th 1954, Sun Studio, Memphis, Tennessee

Blue Moon Of Kentucky - Recorded July 5th 1954, Sun Studio, Memphis, Tennessee

RCA 47  6420        Black Label – Dog On Top

 Side A.  Heartbreak Hotel

Side B.  I Was The One

 Heartbreak Hotel made Billboard's Hot 100 chart on February 22 1956, it became Elvis' first national number 1, it remained on the chart  for twenty seven weeks and stayed at number 1 for seven weeks. On the same day it reached number 1 on the Country charts also for twenty seven weeks. It made the Rhythm & Blues chart on April 4th 1956 at the number 5 slot, it remained on this chart for thirteen weeks. I Was The One reached number 23 on the Hot 100 chart on February 29th 1956, and stayed on the chart for sixteen weeks.

This 45 was released in the UK in March 1956 on His Master's Voice Label, catalogue No.7m 385, it had gold lettering on a purple label, and was later done in silver lettering and reached the number 2 slot. 

 

Matrix numbers: G2WW-0209-1S A1 / G2WW-0218-1S A1

Some of the early pressings have the misspelling of the writer Axton as Axpon this may have only existed less than a month. The original recording information from January 10th 1956 has the writer correctly spelled as Axton. A later 1S pressing is corrected. The March release of Elvis' Golden Records has Axton correctly spelled. This is a very tough variation to find.

Released in 1956 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as Heartbreak Hotel / I Was The One.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Heartbreak Hotel / I Was The One on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

 Heartbreak Hotel - Recorded January 10th 1956, RCA Studio’s Nashville, Tennessee

I Was The One - Recorded January 11th 1956, RCA Studio’s Nashville, Tennessee

I Need Your Love Tonight - Recorded 10th June 1958, RCA Studio B, Nashville. 

RCA 47  7600                        Black Label – Dog On Top

 

Side A.  A Big Hunk O’Love

Side B.  My Wish Came True

 

A Big Hunk O'Love was placed number 1 on June 23rd 1959 on the Hot 100 chart and stayed on the chart for fourteen weeks. The Rhythm & Blues chart had this song at the number 10 on August 2nd, it remained on this chart for seven weeks. The Hot 100 placed My Wish Came True at the number 12 on July 7th, it stayed on the chart for eleven weeks, the Rhythm & Blues chart listed this track at the number 15 slot, it stayed on the chart for three weeks.


Number 4 was the position this 45 held in July 1959 in the UK, released on the RCA label, catalogue No. RCA 1136, it has a black label and a triangular centre, and later released with round centers. 

Matrix numbers: J2WW-3254-1S A6 / H2PW-5524-2S A5

Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as A Big Hunk O' Love / My Wish Came True on the Gold Standard Series label.
