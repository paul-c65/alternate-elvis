RCA 47  6420        Black Label – Dog On Top

 Side A.  Heartbreak Hotel

Side B.  I Was The One

 

Heartbreak Hotel made Billboard's Hot 100 chart on February 22nd 1956, it became Elvis' first national number 1, it remained on the chart  for twenty seven weeks and stayed at number 1 for seven weeks. On the same day it reached number 1 on the Country charts also for twenty seven weeks. It made the Rhythm & Blues chart on April 4th 1956 at the number 5 slot, it remained on this chart for thirteen weeks. I Was The One reached number 23 on the Hot 100 chart on February 29th 1956, and stayed on the chart for sixteen weeks.

This 45 was released in the UK in March 1956 on His Master's Voice Label, catalogue No.7m 385, it had gold lettering on a purple label, and was later done in silver lettering and reached the number 2 slot.

 

Matrix numbers: G2WW-0209-1S A1 / G2WW-0218-1S A1

Some of the early pressings have the misspelling of the writer Axton as Axpon this may have only existed less than a month. The original recording information from January 10th 1956 has the writer correctly spelled as Axton. A later 1S pressing is corrected. The March release of Elvis' Golden Records has Axton correctly spelled. This is a very tough variation to find.

Released in 1956 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as Heartbreak Hotel / I Was The One.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as Heartbreak Hotel / I Was The One on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

 Heartbreak Hotel - Recorded January 10th 1956, RCA Studio’s Nashville, Tennessee

I Was The One - Recorded January 11th 1956, RCA Studio’s Nashville, Tennessee

 

Musicians:-

Elvis Presley  - Vocals, Guitar

Scotty Moore, Chet Atkins - Guitar

Bill Black - Bass

D.J. Fontana - Drums

Floyd Cramer - Piano

Gordon Stoker, Ben Speer, Brock Speer – Vocals

Release Date:- January 27th 1956

 Billboard Chart Position:- US:- 1 / UK:- 2
