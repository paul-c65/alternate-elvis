RCA 47  6637        Black Label – Dog On Top

Side A.  I Got A Woman

Side B.  I’m Counting On You

This single never charted 

Elvis warmed up at the Nashville sessions with a song from his live set, Ray Charles's "I Got A Woman", it is passionately sung and has a rockabilly feel to it with some drum inconsistencies and a slight echo going back to his Sun recordings.

The country ballad "I'm Counting On You" dates from those first RCA sessions in Nashville, and Elvis' great vocals proved he could carry of straight country songs, Steve Sholes new this from the start, that's why he had alerted RCA's marketing team about the singers cross-genre potential.

 

Matrix numbers: G2WW-0208-2S A1 / G2WW-0211-2S A1

Released in 1956 by RCA Records on 78 RPM as I Got A Woman / I'm Counting On You.
Re-released by RCA Records on 45 RPM as I Got A Woman / I'm Counting On You on the Gold Standard Series label.

Recording Dates

I’m Counting On You - Recorded January 10th 1956, RCA Studio’s, Nashville, Tennessee

I Got A Woman - Recorded January 11th 1956, RCA Studio’s, Nashville, Tennessee

 

Musicians:-

Elvis Presley  - Vocals, Guitar

Scotty Moore - Guitar

Chet Atkins - Guitar

Bill Black - Bass

Johnny Bernero, D.J. Fontana - Drums

Floyd Cramer, Shorty Long - Piano

Gordon Stoker, Ben & Brock Speer – Vocals

 Release Date:- August 31st 1956

 Chart Position:- US:-  / UK:-
